Potato Late Blight Phytophthora infestans

Symptoms of Damage

1. Irregularly shaped spreading brown lesions on leaves with distinctive white fluffy sporulation at lesion margins on the underside of the leaf in wet conditions. In dry condition the lesions dry up and go dark brown with collapsed tissue
2. water-soaked dark green to brown lesions on stems also with characteristic white sporulation later in infection leaves and petioles completely rotted; severely affected plants may have an slightly sweet distinctive odor
3. red-brown firm lesions on tubers extending several centimeters into tissue
4. lesions may be slightly sunken in appearance and often lead to secondary bacterial rots.

Remedy

Control depends on a multifaceted approach with importance of certain practices changing based on geographic location:
1. destroy infected tubers
2. destroy any volunteer plants
3. application of appropriate fungicide to potato hills at emergence
4. time watering to reduce periods of leaf wetness
e.g. water early to allow plant to dry off during the day; plant resistant varieties; apply appropriate protective fungicide if disease is forecast in area
