Grape

Soil and Climate

1. Sandy to clayey and loamy soil with good drainage and irrigation facilities is suitable for the cultivation of Grapes.
2. Soils having pH value from 6.5 to 7.5 are most suitable.
3. In its natural habitat, the crop bears fruit during the hot and dry period and undergoes dormancy during the period of severe cold. It tolerates frost during resting stage but is very susceptible during growing period.
4. Temperature ranging from 15-350 C is ideal for shoot growth and normal physiological processes of the grapevine. Vines do not grow and fruit well when the temperature falls below 100 C.
5. Locations where the annual rainfall does not exceed 900 mm. are ideal for its cultivation.

Time of Sowing

The best season for planting the rooted cuttings of cultivated varieties in the main field is September-October whereas for rootstocks it is February-March.

Manure and Fertilizers

nitrogen-rich grapevine fertilizers (such as urea, ammonium nitrate and ammonium sulfate) should be applied after the vine has blossomed or when grapes are about ¼ inch (0.5 cm.) across .

Land preparation

Land is leveled by a tractor or bulldozer as per the requirement, soil type and gradient.  In case of drip irrigation, leveling need not be perfect.
The size of the plot will vary with the type of training system used. In case of bower and telephone or “T” trellis the ideal size could be 60 X 80 m. and 90 X 120 m. respectively.
